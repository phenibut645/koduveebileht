Minimum Viable Product is a test version of a product, service or service with a minimum set of functions (sometimes even one), which is of value to the end user. MVPs are designed to test the hypothesis and test the viability of the intended product, how valuable it will be and what it will be required in the market. The results of the minimum-viable test and the feedback from the target audience help to understand whether the project should be developed further, what changes should be made to the strategy and what should be left in its original form. The usefulness of MVP development is evidenced by examples of large companies so far. For example, Daniel Eck and Martin Laurentson launched a small service in 2006 with a single function, the music streaming. Today, their product, Spotify, is valued at $21 billion, collaborates with large recording studios, and has 50 million active audiences. In 2008, when the rental of a hotel or accommodation during the trip was a major problem, two enthusiasts decided to approach the issue in an unusual way and to fax their apartment. In fact, it's also the MVP that tested the main function. The experiment has shown that the product will be demanded, and Airbnb is now one of the largest short-term rental sites.
